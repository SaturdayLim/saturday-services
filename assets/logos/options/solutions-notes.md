# Saturday Solutions / Saturday Night Games — Logo Options

One shared mark, two uses (printed Solutions vertical + digital Night Games hub).
Shared grammar: geometric S skeleton, 45° language, optical precision, obsidian stage
(#0B0C10), Neon Yellow (#FFE600) as signal only, titanium/chrome neutrals.

viewBox 512×512 · ≥48-unit margins · filled paths only · no gradients/strokes/filters.

## Option A — "Hex Path S"
- **Concept:** An S traced by 9 flat-top hexagonal board tiles (top bar 2 · left connector ·
  middle bar 3 · right connector · bottom bar 2), like squares on a game board.
- **Rationale:** Reads as "game path" + literal S; regular hexagons (circumradius 34) keep the
  family's geometric optics. Uniform 8-unit gaps separate tiles so the snake stays legible.
- **Accent:** Only the start tile (top-right) and end tile (bottom-left) are Neon Yellow —
  they mark where the path begins and ends; everything else is titanium.
- **15mm print:** Every tile is 68 units wide (~7.6mm-equivalent chunk); no fine detail, gaps
  survive at 57px. Mono fills all tiles solid — the S silhouette holds at 32px.

## Option B — "Card Back S"
- **Concept:** A 5:7 playing card (corner radius 24) with a double filled-band border frame,
  a bold geometric S emblem centered as the card-back motif, and two diamond pips in
  opposite corners.
- **Rationale:** Premium casino/game-night cue; the S is a 5-rectangle union (48-unit bar
  weight) so it stays chunky and unmistakable. Borders are filled bands, never strokes.
- **Accent:** The S and both diamond pips are Neon Yellow on a titanium card — accent stays
  concentrated on the emblem, not the frame.
- **15mm print:** Card + S are large solid masses; the double border is two ≥8-unit bands that
  merge cleanly rather than disappearing. Mono uses evenodd rings so bands read at 57px.

## Option C — "Pip S"
- **Concept:** A poker-chip disc (chrome rim + titanium face) with 8 chunky edge-dash arcs,
  and inside, an S spelled out as a dot-matrix of 11 bold circular pips (radius 24 —
  dice/domino language).
- **Rationale:** Most "game-night" of the three; the pip grid nods to dice while the chip nods
  to casino elegance. 16-unit gaps between pips keep the S crisp.
- **Accent:** Start pip (top-left) and end pip (bottom-right) of the S reading path are Neon
  Yellow; the other 9 pips and the chip are neutral.
- **15mm print:** Pips are 48-unit circles and edge dashes are thick sectors — no hairlines.
  Mono renders the rim as an evenodd ring plus solid pips; the S dot-pattern survives at 57px.
